Sadie Sink Was Once This Famous Broadway Character (And Other Memorable Roles Besides Stranger Thing

July 28, 2024
blog

Sadie Sink has seen her career skyrocket in the last few years, mostly thanks to her role in the Netflix sci-fi horror drama series, Stranger Things.

The 20-year-old plays the character known as Max in the show and has featured in 26 out of 34 episodes so far. With only one season left, Max’s fate is not exactly known, and even Sink has been vocal on this state of limbo that her character is in.

Either way, the actress can look back on her time on Stranger Things with pride, although it has not always been smooth sailing. At one point, she was put in an awkward situation by creators Matt and Ross Duffer, an episode that enraged the public against the brothers.

Sink has managed to come through unscathed, however, and continues to build on an impressive career. As well as playing Max on Stranger Things, here are some of her other most memorable roles so far, including a famous character on Broadway.

9 Annie (Annie)

Sadie Sink has been acting since she was 11 and honed her craft on stage before she made her foray into screen work. One of her very early roles was the titular character in the popular Broadway musical play, Annie.

She first played the character in a regional production, before making her Broadway debut at the Palace Theatre in 2012.

8 Maxine 'Max' Mayfield (Stranger Things)

It would be remiss to talk about Sadie Sink’s most memorable roles and fail to mention the biggest of her career so far. She joined the cast of Stranger Things as Max at the beginning of Season 2 and has featured as a regular ever since.

Together with her colleagues on the show, Sink has earned two SAG Award nominations for “Outstanding Performance by an Ensemble in a Drama Series.”

7 Suzanne Ballard (American Odyssey)

Although Max is Sadie Sink’s standout television role so far, she has been a main cast member on a TV show before. In 2015, she appeared in 11 out of 13 episodes of American Odyssey, an action series that aired on NBC.

Sink portrayed a character known as Suzanne Ballard. The show was canceled after just one season.

6 Ziggy Berman (Fear Street Trilogy)

Besides stage and TV, Sadie Sink has also made her mark on the big screen. One of her most notable roles in the movies is Ziggy Berman, in the last two installments of the teen slasher film trilogy, Fear Street.

In both movies, Sink plays a character called Ziggy Berman. The role saw her nominated for an MTV Movie & TV Award this year.

5 Young Queen Elizabeth II (The Audience)

Sadie Sink’s last Broadway role was a huge one, as she starred alongside Helen Mirren in a stage production titled The Audience. With the legendary actress playing reigning Queen Elizabeth II, Sink had the distinct honor of stepping into the shoes of a younger version of the monarch.

Mirren would later win a Tony for her work in the play.

4 Haley (Eli)

Sadie Sink played cameo roles in the movies Chuck and The Glass Castle to kick off her big screen career in 2016 and 2017 respectively. Two years later, she had an even more meaningful part in a film, when she landed a central, supporting role in Ciarán Foy’s horror film, Eli.

Sink was cast in the part of a character called Haley.

3 Tween Girl (Unbreakable Kimmy Schmidt)

Though barely out of her teenage years, Sadie Sink has already proven her versatility. Fewer roles could back this argument up better than her cameo as a tween girl on the Netflix sitcom, Unbreakable Kimmy Schmidt.

Sink only appeared in one episode, titled Kimmy Sees a Sunset.

2 Susan Waverly (White Christmas)

Susan Waverly in a 2011 production of the musical play White Christmas was officially Sadie Sink’s first ever professional acting role. Although the production was not universally well received, it opened the door to other gigs on stage for Sink and arguably, to all other roles that she has taken on since.

As well as episodes of The Americans and Blue Blood, she also featured in a short film titled All Too Well, which was written and directed by musician Taylor Swift in 2021.

1 Sadie Sink’s Upcoming Roles

Although there is a chance that she might not return to Season 5 of Stranger Things, Sadie Sink’s career is guaranteed to keep on growing. The big screen appears to be her main focus moving forward, at least in the interim future.

Sink has already filmed for two upcoming movies, titled The Whale and Dear Zoe. Both films are in post-production and will see the actress feature in main roles.
