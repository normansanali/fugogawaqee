Who is Naoya Inoue?

Naoya Inoue is a well-known Japanese boxer. He has been in the news because he won the WBC and WBO junior featherweight titles.

He is fondly known as “The Monster.” He was born on 10 April 1993 in Zama, Kanagawa, Japan.

His height is 5 feet, 5 inches, or 165 cm. He is currently 30 years old.

He became an undisputed champion at bantamweight in his last contest and is now a unified champion at 122lbs, the fourth division in which he’s held world titles after tepping up for his first bout at super-bantamweight, Fulton Inoue was challenging the acknowledged top boxer in the division without a tune-up bout.

Naoya and his wife were high school sweethearts who eventually married and had three children together.

His career

Naoya Inoue’s boxing career started with a fruitful amateur career in which he amassed an astounding 75-6 record with 48 knockouts. In 2009, he won both the Japanese Junior National Championships and the Japanese Interscholastic Athletic Meeting.

He won the bronze medal in the Asian Youth Championships in 2010 and competed in the AIBA Youth World Championships, making it to the third round of the preliminary competition.

Naoya Inoue made his professional debut in 2012 after joining Ohashi Boxing Gym. After defeating Ryoichi Taguchi, Inoue went on to win the Japanese light flyweight championship.

Inoue defeated Adrián Hernández in his sixth professional contest to win the WBC light flyweight title. He kept up his impressive form and won the WBO junior bantamweight championship. Inoue repeatedly defended his championships, displaying his amazing strength and boxing prowess.

After moving up to bantamweight, Naoya Inoue competed in the World Boxing Super Series and earned the WBA “Regular” title. By beating Michael Dasmarinas, Inoue expanded his collection of titles and continued to rule the sport.

Later, he accomplished the amazing accomplishment of unifying the bantamweight division. Most recently, Inoue moved up to the super bantamweight division and unified the titles by defeating Paul Butler.
