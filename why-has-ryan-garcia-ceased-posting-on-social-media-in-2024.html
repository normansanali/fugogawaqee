Concerns Over Mental Well-being

Bizarre Social Media Posts

In recent weeks, Ryan Garcia's social media posts have raised concerns about his mental state. His account shared a series of worrying posts, including references to 'demons', 'UFOs', and claims of being 'heavily attacked'.

These posts, coupled with earlier ones alleging being raped as a child and witnessing 'human trafficking', have led to widespread speculation about his mental health.

History of Mental Health Battles

Ryan Garcia has a history of being open about his battles with depression and anxiety.

In 2021, he spoke about pulling out of a fight due to his mental health struggles, and in the recent series of posts, he addressed concerns about his well-being, assuring fans and family that he is okay.

Allegations of Hacking and Online Negativity

Hacking Speculations

Following the string of bizarre posts, fans and boxing community members have speculated that Ryan Garcia's social media accounts might have been hacked.

Impact of Online Negativity

In addition to the hacking speculations, Garcia cited online negativity as a factor in his decision to step away from social media.

He expressed feeling unloved and hurt due to the hate he perceives on social media, leading him to take a break from posting until after his fight with Devin Haney.

Professional Focus and Boxing Match Preparation

Shielding from Distractions

Ryan Garcia's decision to take a break from social media indicates a desire to shield himself from negative distractions and focus on his upcoming fight against Haney.

This move reflects his commitment to maintaining his mental focus and physical preparation for the significant bout.

Financial Incentives and Future Matches

Garcia's aspirations for significant financial gains in boxing have also been highlighted.

He aims to secure high-stakes matches against prominent boxers like Gervonta Davis, Teofimo Lopez, and Jake Paul, emphasizing the importance of winning his upcoming fight against Haney to progress towards these lucrative opportunities.

Reactions and Concerns from Peers and Family

Concerns from Boxing Peers

Notable figures in the boxing community, including KSI and Eddie Hearn, have expressed concerns about Ryan Garcia's recent behavior, with KSI suggesting that the upcoming fight with Haney should be called off to get Garcia help.

Conversely, Haney has insisted that their world-title contest is still on, accusing Garcia of 'playing crazy' to promote their fight.

Family and Ex-Wife's Perspectives

All in all

Ryan Garcia's decision to cease posting on social media in 2024 is influenced by a combination of factors.

As the boxing world eagerly awaits his showdown with Devin Haney, the public remains attentive to any developments regarding Ryan Garcia's well-being and future in the sport.
