Decoding Taylor Swift’s ‘Gorgeous’

Taylor Swift has been synonymous with catchy pop tunes, jaw-dropping music videos and heartbreaking lyrics. Her third single from her sixth studio album – Reputation – ‘Gorgeous – has set the hearts of her fans, the media, and critics racing. The song entered the Billboard Hot 100 chart at No. 13, meanwhile, the music video for the song has raaked up over 62 million views.

The song, which is written by Swift herself, is laced with witty punchlines and groovy beats. But just as with many of Swift’s lyrics, the song has deeper layers that unveil the journey of Swift’s personal life and how she has experienced love. So, let’s dive deep into ‘Gorgeous’ and decode the meanings behind the song.

“Gorgeous”- The Connotations

The opening line of the song, “Gorgeous, you should take it as a compliment that I got drunk and made fun of the way you talk,” is enough to grab your attention. In the chorus, Swift confesses, ‘And all at once, you are the one I have been waiting for. Kings of the palace, you’re my American boy, American boy.’”

The ‘Gorgeous’ lyrics may seem simple, but the connotations are deeper than you might imagine. In an interview with iHeartRadio, Swift revealed that the song is “about the feeling that you get when you meet someone that you really want in your life, and then all of the sudden, that person isn’t your own.”

Swift’s Personal Life and ‘Gorgeous’

Taylor Swift has had a very public career, her personal life, and relationships have always been in the spotlight. Her love life has remained a talking point, leading to many of her previous albums. Swift’s previous relationships have been well documented, and the song ‘Gorgeous’ is believed to further explore her past love experiences.

One of the most talked-about aspects of ‘Gorgeous’ is the appearance of James Reynolds, the two-year-old daughter of Ryan Reynolds and Blake Lively. In the song, you can clearly hear the word “gorgeous,” which is spoken by Reynolds.

Swift and Lively have been friends for years, and Swift has been photographed spending time with the family on numerous occasions. It is believed that Reynolds’s appearance in the song could have been a sign of their friendship and could also suggest that Swift was present when Reynolds said the word ‘gorgeous.’

The Inspiration behind ‘Gorgeous’

Taylor Swift’s inspiration behind ‘Gorgeous’ came from a very personal place. The chorus of the song was written two years before the creation of the full song. In an interview with Time Magazine, Swift revealed that the song was actually about her boyfriend, actor Joe Alwyn.

“I think it’s just the feeling that you get when you meet somebody that you really want in your life and then all of a sudden, that person isn’t yours,” Swift said. “There’s a sense of longing and sadness and confusion that shows up right after that one spark. Immediately after you meet someone, there’s sometimes a terrible feeling of ‘Oh God, can I make this person like me as much as I like them?’”

The Bottom-line

The magic behind ‘Gorgeous’ is how it manages to pack an emotional punch while still remaining upbeat and catchy. Taylor Swift has always been a master of writing songs that speak to the heart of her listeners, and ‘Gorgeous’ is an excellent example of that.

The song’s connotations and lyrics reveal her experience with love and relationships, and she masterfully portrays this in her music video, which is bright and colorful.

Swift’s ‘Gorgeous’ is more than just a catchy tune – it is a testament to her songwriting ability and her ability to connect with her listeners on a deeper level.

It’s no wonder that she is one of the most celebrated singers in the world. So, sit back, plug in and enjoy “Gorgeous” by Taylor Swift!
