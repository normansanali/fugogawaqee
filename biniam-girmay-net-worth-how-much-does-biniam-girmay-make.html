Biniam Girmay Hailu born April 2, 2000 is an Eritrean professional road cyclist who currently competes on the UCI WorldTeam Intermarché–Wanty–Gobert Matériaux.

He became the first Black African cyclist to win a Grand Tour stage after winning stage 10 in a reduced bunch sprint in the 2022 Giro d’Italia.

Biniam joined the World Cycling Centre in 2018 for his second junior year after discovering cycling through his cousin Meron Teshome.

He won the road race, time trial, and team time trial that year, making him Africa’s triple junior cycling champion.

He also defeated Remco Evenepoel in the opening stage of Aubel–Thimister–Stavelot.
