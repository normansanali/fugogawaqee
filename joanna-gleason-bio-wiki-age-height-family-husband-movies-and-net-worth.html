Joanna Gleason Biography

Joanna Gleason is a Canadian actress and singer who was born and raised in Toronto, Canada. She is famously known for originating the role of the Baker’s Wife in Stephen Sondheim’s Into the Woods(1987) where she won the Tony Award for Best Actress in a Musical. She has also been a teacher, holding classes and workshops all over the country.

Joanna Actress

Joanna made her Broadway debut in 1977 in I Love My Wife, for which she was honored with a Theatre World Award. Her other Broadway credits include Tom Stoppard’s The Real Thing, Peter Nichols’ A Day in the Death of Joe Egg, Nick & Nora, Into the Woods, Dirty Rotten Scoundrels, and The Cartells.

Joanna’s film and television career started in 1977 with her first appearance on her father’s program Let’s Make A Deal. She appeared as a supporting character in Hello, Larryin 1979, a role she held throughout the oft-maligned sitcom’s two-season run. In 1986 she had film roles in Hannah and Her Sisters and Heartburn. In the television movie Still the Beaver she played Beaver’s ex-wife, Kimberly.

Joanna has been cast in The Good Wife, Blue Bloods, and The Newsroom, and The Wedding Planner. She also portrayed Joan Silver on Temporarily Yours (1997). Joanna starred in the Lifetime series Oh Baby as Charlotte from 1998 to 2000, also directing episodes of this show. From 2014 to 2016 she was cast in 6 episodes of the Canadian black comedy series Sensitive Skin as Veronica,
